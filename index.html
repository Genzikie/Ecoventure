ATMOSPHERIC TEMPERATURE IS RISING!

The Earth's atmosphere serves as the invisible blanket that keeps the planet warm. When the Sun's heat enters the atmosphere, some of its rays are reflected back into space and some are absorbed by the atmosphere.

But since the Industrial Revolution, humans began to produce large amounts of greenhouse gases that polluted and covered the atmosphere preventing the radiated heat from escape. The heat trapped made the planet's atmospheric temperature even warmer.

MELTING OF GLACIERS
When snowfalls and snowflakes accumulate, compress and recrystallize, massive blocks of slowly moving ice---glaciers---are formed. Most glaciers were formed during the last ice age. It is also a home to many terestrial and aquatic species. It's been more than half a century since glaciers started to melt due to global warming. As melting of glaciers accelerate, drastic repercussions are taking place.
PREVALENCE OF DISEASE
Climate is a very influential factor to emergence and reemergence of infectious diseases. Changes in climate patterns can directly affect the transmission of climate-sensitive diseases like mosquito-borne diseases such as malaria, dengue and viral encephalitides. Higher sea surface temperature and level can also increase the watre-borne diseases and toxin-related illnesses and poisoning.
SUBMERGING ISLANDS
Rising sea levels can make islands disappear. Because of climate change, sea water now threatens to swallow coastlines, islands and even small countries. Aquatic species and habitats are also extremely vulnerable to this. People living in disappearing lands would experience other climate change impacts and resort to evacuation, and there will be climate-change refugees.
SEA LEVEL RISE
Global warming leads to melting of land ice that adds more water into the oceans. Also, more heat is being absorbed by the water which causes its expanding, leading to sea level rise. This impact greatly affects our land, specifically the coastal areas. This impact causes soil erosion, farmland destruction, islands submerging, loss of animal habitats and leads to stronger storms and flooding.
WATER SCARCITY
Changes in climate patterns leading to extreme weather events cause droughts and floods. Because of this, water cycle is also affected. There are times that people have no access to water because of drought, and there are times that intense flooding destroy resources, crops planted, and increases chances of water contamination. Sea level rise and melting of glaciers are also factors contributing to this impact.
INCREASED STRESS AND DISEASE

Researches show that climate change is also associated with mental and physical health related issues.

Disasters brought about by climate change, flooding, droughts, high temperature and heavy rains, for example, may cause post-traumatic stress disorders and increased anxiety to people who are either directly or indirectly affected by it. When people are forced to leave a certain place because of the effects of climate change, they are likely to experience acculturation stress.

Disasters can also bring fear, diseases and physical harm to anyone.

INCREASED MORBIDITY FROM DIARRHEAL DISEASE
Another impact of climate change that threatens public health is the increasing morbidity from diarrheal disease. While everyone is at risk, children, people with underlying health conditions, pregnant women, elders, poor and disadvantaged communities are considered to be the most vulnerable to this. This catastrophic impact can result to millions of climate-change related cases.
INCREASED ENDEMIC MORTALITY
The increase in temperature due to global warming will vary by region, so will the effects. Distribution of infectious diseases and other climate-change related diseases will also be geographically dependent which tells us that there can be places with high chance of survival and places that may receive greatest threat of increased endemic mortality.
RISE IN COASTAL WATER TEMPERATURE WHICH WOULD EXACERBATE TOXICITY OF CHOLERA
Due to climate change, oceans are absorbing more heat and becomes warmer, and sea levels are also rising. A combination of these impacts will lead to increased flooding, water contamination to coastal areas that worsens or exacerbates cholera epidemics.
MASS MIGRATION
With the extreme weather conditions and disastrous consequences of climate change, mass migration may be the most promising strategy for the people who are aiming for survival. Migrants/refugees are expected to increase in number if climate change impacts are not alleviated immediately.
SUFFERING OF CROP PRODUCTION
Impacts of the climate change including change in temperature, moisture, carbon dioxide concentration, weather conditions, droughts, flooding, and water supply will also affect food production. Growing and harvesting seasons will be longer than usual and there will be plants that might not be able to adapt as necessary.
CUT REVENUES AND INCREASED SPENDING NEEDS,WORSENING PUBLIC FINANCES
The destructive impacts of climate change can directly cause imbalance to public finance. With the increasing number of calamities, diseases, foods and water security, governments are forced to spend much on rehabilitation and disaster reliefs operations. On the other hand, climate change impacts also lead to lower revenues as it greatly affects tourism and agriculture.
